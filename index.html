What is the difference between '==' and '===' operators? Give an example of when you will use each type of operator?

'==' is loose equality in JS. It is used to check the equality of two operands regardless of their type.

On the other hand, '===' is strict equality in JS. It compares both the values and types of the operands.

I would use '==' if i want to check "null" and "undefined" at once with value == null. Other than that, strict equality should be preferred due to it being easier to predict.
